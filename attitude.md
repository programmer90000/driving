# Attitude - Driving Theory Notes

## 1. Safe Driving Mindset
- **Priority**: Safety of all road users comes first
- **Responsibility**: You are responsible for your vehicle and actions
- **Patience**: Allow extra time for journeys, don't rush
- **Courtesy**: Be considerate to other road users

## 2. Aggressive Driving (Road Rage)
- **Never react** to aggression from other drivers
- **Don't make eye contact** with aggressive drivers
- **Don't tailgate (Drive to close to the vehicle in front)** - it intimidates other drivers
- **Avoid flashing headlights** to show annoyance
- **Don't use the horn** aggressively
- **Stay calm** and focused on safe driving

## 3. Competitive Behavior
- **Don't race** other vehicles
- **Don't compete** for parking spaces or road position
- **Avoid challenging** other drivers' actions
- **Resist pressure** from passengers to drive faster or more aggressively

## 4. Consideration for Others
- **Vulnerable road users**: Give extra space to cyclists, motorcyclists, horse riders
- **Pedestrians**: Always be prepared to stop for them
- **Learner drivers**: Be patient and don't intimidate them
- **Large vehicles**: Understand they need more space and time to manoeuvre

## 5. Sharing the Road
- **Don't block** junctions or box junctions
- **Allow merging** in traffic queues (zip-merging)
- **Let people out** from side roads when safe
- **Bus lanes**: Obey the times and don't block them

## 6. Key Attitudes to Avoid
- **Impatience** - rushing, overtaking dangerously
- **Intimidation** - driving too close, aggressive gestures
- **Selfishness** - putting your convenience before safety
- **Showing off** - speeding, rapid acceleration

## 7. Positive Attitudes
- **Calm** - even in heavy traffic or delays
- **Predictable** - signal clearly and in good time
- **Legal** - obey speed limits and road signs
- **Alert** - aware of other road users' needs
