When I pick up the clutch a little bit when starting the car, it is called the biting point

Signalling up/ down is left of the wheel. Wipers are right side of the wheel

Signalling up is right, signalling down is left

To change gears, push the clutch down quickly. Bring it up slowly

Stay a cm away from the kerb while driving

When I go down a gear, go into neutral first

When going downhill, use the break to control the car. When going uphill, use the clutch to control the car

Before doing anything, I need to check the mirrors and blind spot

# How to turn from the main junction into the side road
1. Look at all of the mirrors
2. Signal
3. Position slightly to the right
4. Brake
5. Look at all of the mirrors again
6. Once the road is clear, turn



# How to start the car
1. Push the seat forward or backwards until I am in a good position
2. Fasten the seatbelt
3. Adjust the mirrors. I should be able to see almost all of the back window from the main mirror. I should be able to see the car handles and the road from the side mirrors
4. Make sure the gearstick is in neutral
5. Make sure the handbrake is up
6. Twist the key to start the engine
7. Select the turn signal
8. Press the clutch pedal fully
9. While holding the clutch pedal, move the gearstick fully left and then up for the first gear. Keep my foot fully planted on the clutch pedal
10. Look in all of the mirrors to ensure no cars are coming. Turn my head right to look in the blind spot
11 . Lift my left foot slightly, until the car reaches biting point
12. Push the brake pedal down and then push the handbrake down 
13. Let go of the brake pedal and turn the car. The car should begin moving slowly
14. Begin to press the accelerator and let go of the clutch
15. Steer smoothly to enter your lane or the flow of traffic
16. Cancel your turn signal
17. As you gain speed, shift up through the gears

# How to stop the car temporarily
1. Let go of the gas pedal
2. Press the clutch pedal fully. Slowly press the brake pedal until the car stops
3. Keep my foot on the brake pedal until I want to go

# How to park the car
1. Let go of the gas
2. If the car doesn't stop on its own, press the brake slightly
3. Once the car has stopped, keep my foot on the brake
4. Put the handbrake up
5. Move the gearstick to neutral
6. Let go of the parking brake
7. Turn off the engine
