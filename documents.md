# Documents for the Driving Theory Test Complete Notes

## Driving Licence Requirements

### Legal Requirement to Drive
    * You **MUST** have a valid driving licence for the category of vehicle you are driving.
    * You **MUST** complete and send the relevant application form to the Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency (DVLA) to obtain your first licence.
    * You **MUST** meet the **minimum eyesight requirements** (read a number plate from 20 metres). You MUST wear glasses or contact lenses every time you drive if you need them to meet this standard.

### Provisional Licence
    * You **MUST** have a valid **provisional driving licence** before you can drive on public roads.
    * When driving with a provisional licence:
        * You **MUST** be supervised by a person who is at least **21 years old** and has held (and still holds) a full licence for that category of vehicle for at least **3 years**.
        * You **MUST** display **L plates** in a visible place on the front and back of the vehicle.
        * You **CANNOT** drive on the motorway.

### Applying for Your Full Licence
    * After passing your practical driving test, the examiner will give you a **pass certificate**.
    * Your provisional licence will be taken and a full licence will be sent to you automatically. You can start driving immediately; you do not need to wait for the physical card to arrive.
    * You do not need to display L plates once you have passed your test.

## Vehicle Documents

### Key Documents for a Vehicle
    When driving, you **MUST** be able to produce your driving licence, a valid insurance certificate, and a valid MOT certificate (if the vehicle requires one). You may need to produce these at a police station within 7 days.
    * **Driving Licence:** The physical photocard.
    * **Vehicle Tax:** It is illegal to drive a vehicle without tax. You can tax a vehicle online or at a Post Office.
    * **Insurance:** You **MUST** have at least **third-party insurance** before driving any vehicle on the road. It covers injury to another person, damage to someone else's property, and injury to your passengers. It does **not** cover damage to your own vehicle.
    * **MOT Certificate:**
        * A vehicle **MUST** have a valid MOT certificate if it is **over 3 years old**.
        * It is a test of the vehicle's safety and environmental standards, valid for one year.
        * You can drive a vehicle to a pre-booked MOT test appointment without a valid MOT.

### Vehicle Registration Certificate (V5C)
    * This is the document issued by the DVLA to the vehicle's registered keeper.
    * It is **not** proof of ownership, but it names the registered keeper.
    * You **MUST** inform the DVLA immediately via the V5C form if:
        * You change your name or address.
        * The vehicle is modified (e.g., engine conversion).
        * The vehicle is permanently exported, scrapped, or sold.

## Notifying the DVLA

### When You MUST Inform the DVLA
    You have a legal obligation to inform the DVLA about changes. Failure to do so can result in a fine.
    * **Changes to Your Health:**
        * You **MUST** inform the DVLA if you have a medical condition that could affect your driving (e.g., epilepsy, strokes, severe mental health problems, visual impairments not corrected by glasses).
        * Some conditions require you to **surrender your licence**.
    * **Changes to Your Vehicle:**
        * When you **sell or transfer** your vehicle, you **MUST** inform the DVLA immediately by filling in the relevant section of the V5C log book. The buyer will send their details to the DVLA.
        * When you **buy a vehicle**, you **MUST** inform the DVLA and tax the vehicle immediately. The vehicle tax does **not** transfer with the vehicle.
    * **Changes to Your Personal Details:**
        * You **MUST** inform the DVLA if you change your name, title, or address. Your driving licence, vehicle tax, and V5C must all be updated.

## Selling, Scrapping, and Buying

### Selling or Transferring a Vehicle
    * As the seller, you **MUST**:
        1. Fill in section 9 of the V5C registration certificate (the "New Keeper Supplement").
        2. Give the green "New Keeper's Details" (V5C/2) section to the new keeper – this is their proof of purchase and allows them to tax the vehicle.
        3. Send the rest of the V5C to the DVLA to inform them you are no longer the keeper.
    * The new keeper is responsible for sending the V5C to the DVLA with their details to become the new registered keeper.

### When a Vehicle is Scrapped
    * You **MUST** inform the DVLA when you scrap your vehicle.
    * Give the full V5C certificate to the scrap yard or authorised treatment facility. They will issue a "Certificate of Destruction" and inform the DVLA on your behalf.

## Legal Obligations and Penalties

### Producing Documents for the Police
    * If a police officer asks, you **MUST** be able to produce your:
        * Driving Licence
        * Certificate of Insurance
        * MOT Test Certificate (if required)
    * You may be given a **producer**, which is a form requiring you to take these documents to a police station within **7 days**.

### Penalties for Document Offences
    * **Driving without a valid licence:** Fine of up to **£1,000**, penalty points, and the vehicle may be seized.
    * **Driving without valid insurance:** **Heavy fine (unlimited)**, 6-8 penalty points, and possible disqualification. The vehicle can be seized and possibly crushed.
    * **Driving without a valid MOT:** Fine of up to **£1,000**. The vehicle may be deemed unroadworthy and can be seized.
    * **Failure to notify the DVLA** of a change of name or address: Can result in a fine of up to **£1,000**.

## Key Things to Remember for the Test

    * The three key documents to drive are: **Licence, Insurance, and MOT (if required)**.
    * You **MUST** inform the DVLA about changes to your **health, address, and when you sell your vehicle**.
    * **Vehicle tax does NOT transfer to the new keeper** when you sell a car.
    * The minimum supervisor age for a provisional licence holder is **21** and they must have held a full licence for **3 years**.
    * **Third-party insurance** is the legal minimum. It covers others, not you.
    * An **MOT is required for vehicles over 3 years old**.
    * You have **7 days** to produce your documents at a police station if requested.
